Mahatma Gandhi was a political and spiritual leader who played a key role in the Indian independence movement. He was known for his nonviolent resistance and advocacy for civil disobedience, and he used these methods to challenge British rule in India. Gandhi was also a leader of the Indian National Congress and was instrumental in shaping the party's policies and strategies. He is widely regarded as a national hero in India and is remembered for his contributions to the country's struggle for independence.
